SYNC PANEL – Help

Engine Room Generator Synchronization Simulator – Sync Panel AMP

1. Introduction – Purpose of the Simulator

Modern marine power plants depend on diesel generators, busbar systems, protective devices and, in many ports, shore-power connections. Safe synchronization, correct load sharing and proper response to abnormal electrical conditions are essential skills for Marine Engineering Officers.

In actual shipboard operation, manual synchronization is not always practiced frequently because many vessels use automatic Power Management Systems. Real practice also involves operational risk, fuel cost, possible blackout, equipment stress and limited opportunity to create controlled fault scenarios. For this reason, this simulator was developed to provide a safe, repeatable and educational environment for understanding generator synchronization and marine power management.

The Sync Panel AMP Simulator reproduces a medium-voltage shipboard power plant operating at 6.6 kV / 60 Hz. It includes two diesel generator sets, a shore-power AMP source, a synchroscope, a clean AC waveform oscilloscope, busbar measurements, load and power factor controls, breaker operation and protective-trip behavior.

This simulator is intended for:

  • Marine Engineers under training
  • Chief Engineers preparing for operational command
  • Electrical Officers involved in shipboard power management
  • Engine Room Simulator classroom instruction
  • Self-training in synchronization and load-sharing principles

2. General Panel Description

The simulator is arranged as a marine main switchboard training panel. It contains:

  • BUS MEASUREMENTS section
  • SYNCHROSCOPE section
  • G1 and G2 diesel generator sections
  • AMP / shore-power section
  • VCB controls, governor controls, reset buttons and trip indicators

3. Busbar Section

  • Bus Voltage: Displays the live busbar voltage. The simulated system is 6.6 kV.
  • Bus Frequency: Displays the actual bus frequency.
  • Total Load Slider: Operator-controlled training input for the total demanded ship load.
  • cosφ Slider: Operator-controlled power factor input used for current and apparent-power calculations.
  • Bus kW and kVA: Show active and apparent power of the busbar load.
  • Bus Current: Calculated from the three-phase power relationship.

4. Diesel Generator Sections – G1 and G2

Each diesel generator section includes:

  • Voltmeter: Generator line-to-line voltage.
  • Power Meter: Active power in kW, including reverse-power region.
  • Ammeter: Generator line current.
  • Frequency Indicator: Generator/bus frequency.
  • Governor Control: Changes the no-load speed/frequency set point.
  • Fuel Index: Training indication of relative fuel command.
  • SFOC: Specific fuel oil consumption indication in g/kWh.
  • Fuel Consumption: Estimated fuel flow in kg/h.
  • VCB Control: Opens or closes the generator breaker.
  • RESET Button: Clears latched protection trips.

5. Governor Droop and Load Sharing

G1 and G2 use a Woodward-style 5% droop training model. The governor lever does not directly command generator kW. It changes the generator no-load frequency set point.

The simplified training relationship is:

P = K × (fset − fbus)

For a 4.5 MW diesel generator at 60 Hz with 5% droop:

Full-load droop = 5% × 60 Hz = 3 Hz
K = 4500 kW / 3 Hz = 1500 kW/Hz

When two diesel generators operate in parallel, the difference between their governor set points determines load sharing. The average of the set points, together with total load, affects bus frequency.

Practical Rule

  • Raise the governor of the generator that must take more load.
  • Lower the governor of the generator that must give away load.
  • Use both governors carefully to transfer load without causing excessive bus frequency change.

6. AMP / Shore-Power Section

The AMP source represents Alternative Maritime Power, also known as shore power, cold ironing, OPS or HVSC.

In this simulator, AMP is not treated as a diesel generator. It has no governor, no fuel index, no SFOC and no fuel-consumption indication. It is modeled as a stiff 60 Hz shore-grid source.

AMP Model

  • Voltage: 6.6 kV AC
  • Frequency: 60 Hz
  • Three-phase supply
  • Rated apparent power: approximately 7.32 MVA
  • Available active power depends on cosφ: Pmax = S × cosφ

At cosφ = 0.80, the AMP can supply approximately 5.85 MW. At cosφ = 1.00, it can supply approximately 7.32 MW.

When connected, the AMP holds the bus at 60 Hz and supplies the residual load after the diesel generator droop contribution is calculated. If the diesel generators attempt to export power toward the shore supply, the simulator treats this as an abnormal AMP export condition.

7. Synchroscope

The synchroscope shows the phase relationship between the selected incoming source and the live busbar. The source selector is placed below the synchroscope and allows selection of:

  • G1
  • OFF
  • G2
  • AMP

The breaker may be closed onto a live bus only when frequency difference and phase angle are within the permitted synchronization window. If an out-of-sync closing is attempted, the simulator produces a protection trip.

8. Clean AC Waveform Oscilloscope

The oscilloscope provides a direct visual comparison of one AC cycle.

  • Green waveform: Busbar AC voltage
  • Red waveform: Selected incoming source voltage

When the green and red waveforms are aligned, the incoming source is in phase with the busbar. When the red waveform is shifted, a phase difference exists. The oscilloscope display is intentionally clean: no sweep-glow or extra visual effect is used, so the trainee can focus only on the two voltage waveforms and the phase relationship.

9. Breaker Closing Logic

  • Dead bus: The first source can be connected without synchronization.
  • Live bus: Synchronization is required before closing another source.
  • Latched trip: Closing is blocked until RESET is pressed.
  • Out-of-sync close: The simulator trips the relevant breakers.

10. Protection Functions

Diesel Generator Protection

  • Reverse Power Alarm: approximately −120 kW
  • Reverse Power Trip: approximately −220 kW after time delay
  • Overcurrent Trip: breaker opens automatically when current exceeds the set limit
  • High Power / Overload: breaker trip when generator operating limits are exceeded

AMP Protection

  • Overload / kVA Limit: trips if the AMP operating envelope is exceeded
  • Overcurrent: trips if AMP current exceeds its rating
  • Export to Shore: trips if the diesel generators attempt to feed power back toward the AMP supply

11. Basic Operating Instructions

To Synchronize G2 to the Bus

  1. Select G2 on the synchronizing selector.
  2. Observe the synchroscope direction and speed.
  3. Use the G2 governor to reduce frequency difference.
  4. Observe the red waveform on the oscilloscope relative to the green bus waveform.
  5. Close VCB 2 only when the synchroscope is in phase and the waveforms are aligned.
  6. After closing, use governors to adjust load sharing.

To Transfer Load Between G1 and G2

  1. Raise the governor of the generator that must take more load.
  2. Lower the governor of the generator that must reduce load.
  3. Observe both kW meters and bus frequency.
  4. Keep bus frequency within an acceptable range during the transfer.

To Connect AMP

  1. Select AMP on the synchronizing selector.
  2. Observe the synchroscope and the oscilloscope.
  3. Close the AMP VCB only when synchronization is correct.
  4. After closing, observe that AMP holds the bus at 60 Hz.
  5. Monitor AMP kW, kVA and current limits.
  6. Avoid diesel generator export toward the AMP source.

To Recover After a Trip

  1. Identify the trip indication.
  2. Confirm the affected VCB is open.
  3. Reduce load or correct the abnormal condition.
  4. Press the corresponding RESET button.
  5. Re-synchronize before reconnecting to a live bus.

12. Training Capabilities

  • Dead-bus generator connection
  • Manual synchronization using a synchroscope
  • Manual synchronization using AC waveform comparison
  • Parallel diesel generator load sharing
  • Governor droop behavior demonstration
  • Bus frequency behavior under changing load
  • Reverse-power trip demonstration
  • Overcurrent and overload trip demonstration
  • AMP shore-power connection
  • AMP residual-load behavior
  • AMP export-trip behavior
  • Reset and recovery after protection trips

13. Educational Note

This simulator is a training tool. It is intended to explain principles and support classroom instruction. It does not replace ship-specific procedures, manufacturer manuals, classification rules or company SMS instructions.

14. Conclusion

The Sync Panel AMP Simulator provides a safe and realistic environment for learning generator synchronization, droop-based load sharing, shore-power operation and protection behavior. The clean oscilloscope helps trainees connect the synchroscope indication with the actual AC voltage phase relationship.
